Building a second brain

Building a second brain

Recently I have discovered the value of offloading information from my mind to my second brain. Inspired by Tiago Forte book Building a Second Brain

Why a second brain?

A second brain is a personal knowledge management system that supports offloading information and improve information retrieval and recall.

Information Overload

In the early days of the internet we had a single flow of information which was email and far less marketing messages. Overtime as the internet evolved we now see our inbox receives a barrage of messages from various sources (Most of it is bulk mail).

  • In addition we all face continous interuptions from the following sources:
    • Email
    • SMS/iMessage/WhatsApp
    • Slack/Teams
    • Social Networks (Facebook, Twitter, etc..)
    • Calendar Notifications
    • YouTube

So with all this information flow how can we identify the signal from the noise?

Common Tools

In Building a Second Brain book we need to identify a way to capture the following. A way to manage our Todo items (Things that we can’t complete immediately), Calendar (upcoming events or appointments), Note taking tool to capture thoughts, ideas and information that resonates with us

  • Todo apps (such as Todoist, Microsoft Todo, Google Tasks, setc..)
  • Calendar (Google Calendar, Outlook, etc..)
  • Note Taking Apps
    • Simple Notes (eg Apple Notes, Google Keep, VS Code, The Archive, Drafts, etc..)
    • Online (Evernote, Notion, Roam, etc..)
    • Local storage (Obsidian, LogSeq, etc..)

Personal Knowledgement

My personal requirements for a PKM tool are as follows:

  • Own your data
  • Support for Forward and Backlinking
  • Support for rich media
  • Search – Needs to be easy to find information
  • Scalable – PKM system needs to be scalable to large amounts of information
  • Provide a mechanism to visualize connections betweeen concepts
  • Tagging
  • Text based (prefer Markdown) – Needs to be timeless
  • Version Control
  • Must work with both Windows, Mac and Linux ecosystem

Personal vs Work

Organize both my Personal and Work world based on P.A.R.A. Structure around my work and personal Projects, Areas, Resources and Archives.

  • Projects are short-term that require most immediate attention

  • Areas of responsibity such as Family , Friends, Co-workers, Interests, Ambitions

  • Resources links, videos that support project, areas and ambitions

  • Archive for information that is not currently related to Projects and Areas or competed Projects

  • My work notes require that capture information that is related to projects and areas of responsibilty. Information must be stored in trusted locations.

    • Tools that are supported include: Teams, One Note, and Microsoft Todo
    • Logseq also allows for local storage of notes and information

  • Personal

    • Todo - Todoist and/or Google Task capture important emails that need follow-up. Process email once and if necessary create a TODO to time when email can be processed
